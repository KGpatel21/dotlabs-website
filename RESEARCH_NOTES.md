# IT Company Website Research — 10 Sites Analysed

Reference notes compiled for the DotLabs website rebuild. The goal was to study how
established mid-sized IT / custom-software firms (peers of **tatvasoft.com**) structure
their sites, present services, write copy, and design for credibility — then apply those
patterns to DotLabs.

Sites studied: **TatvaSoft, Radixweb, Simform, Hidden Brains, OpenXcell, ScienceSoft,
Net Solutions, Daffodil Software, Mindbowser, Clarion Technologies.**

---

## PART A — Per-site notes

### 1. TatvaSoft (tatvasoft.com) — the reference
**Structure / nav:** Mega-menu header. Top-level items: **Services** (dropdown: Custom
Software, AI Development, Web, Dedicated Team, Product Development, eCommerce, Mobile,
Testing & QA, UI/UX), **Technologies** (grouped Backend / Frontend / Mobile / Others),
**Industries** (11 verticals), **Portfolio**, **Insights** (blog), **About** (About Us,
Career, Our Team), **Contact**. Each service/tech/industry is its own dedicated page.
**Hero:** Tagline "Sculpt Your Thoughts To Reality" + one-line value prop + a 4-stat band
(1800+ projects, 25+ yrs, 810+ customers, 36 countries) + certification logos (Microsoft
Partner, CMMI Level 3).
**Homepage section flow:** Hero → "Software Development Company / Serving Start-ups to
Fortune 500" service cards (6, each = icon + title + 1-sentence outcome + arrow link) →
Case Studies (image cards tagged by industry) → "Delivering the Right Solution"
(industry chips) → Technologies band → "Innovation is Right Here" (4 differentiators:
User-Oriented Approach, Agile Decision Making, Team Augmentation, Cloud & DevOps) →
testimonials (named, with company) → "Engineering Culture" → blog → "Why TatvaSoft" (4
reasons: On-demand/Flexible, Cost Benefit, 1080+ experts, Eliminate Risk) → Global Presence
(map, 7 countries) → FAQs.
**Visual style:** Clean, corporate, lots of white space, blue accent, soft card shadows,
icon + hover-fill pattern on service cards, subtle illustration backgrounds.
**Copy / tone:** Confident, outcome-led, enterprise. Service blurbs are a single sentence
naming the benefit ("Translate unique client requirements into software solutions with
premier quality and advanced technologies"). Heavy use of trust proof (CMMI, years,
client counts, country count). Differentiators framed around *how they work*, not just
*what they build*.
**CTAs:** "Hire Us Now", per-service arrow links, "Learn More", contact.
**Takeaways:** Stats band in hero; per-service dedicated pages; a "why us" block AND an
"how we work" block are separate; named testimonials with company; global-presence framing.

## PART B — Cross-cutting patterns (the playbook)

**1. Navigation.** Universal top-level set: **Services · Technologies · Industries ·
Portfolio/Case Studies · About (incl. Team/Careers) · Insights/Blog · Contact.** Services,
technologies, and industries are *dropdowns to dedicated pages*, never anchor jumps. Primary
CTA button ("Hire Us" / "Book a consultation" / "Get a quote") sits top-right.

**2. Hero formula.** Eyebrow ("Software Development Company") → bold benefit headline (often
metaphor: "Sculpt Your Thoughts To Reality") → 1–2 sentence value prop → **stat band**
(projects / years / clients / countries) → certification or client logos → 1–2 CTAs.

**3. Service descriptions — the formula.** Icon + Title + **one outcome-focused sentence**
(benefit, not feature) on cards; then a *dedicated page per service* that expands into:
what it is, what's included (bulleted deliverables), the process, the tech, who it's for,
and a CTA. Verbs used: *translate, engineer, accelerate, modernize, scale, streamline,
automate, integrate.*

**4. "Why us" vs "How we work" are two different blocks.**
- *Why us* = trust reasons (senior team, cost benefit, risk elimination, flexibility,
  ownership, security, long-term partnership).
- *How we work* = the delivery process (discovery → planning → design → build → QA →
  deploy → support), agile cadence, weekly demos, single point of contact, "no surprises."

**5. Trust / credibility stack (used by all).** Years in business · projects delivered ·
client count · countries · retention/satisfaction % · certifications (CMMI, ISO 27001) ·
named-client logos · named testimonials (person + title + company) · awards · Great Place to
Work · partner badges (Microsoft/AWS/Google) · global-presence map.

**6. About-page anatomy.** Origin story → mission/values → what makes us different →
**leadership team with photos + bios + credentials** → culture → milestones/timeline →
global presence → CTA. (Daffodil & Clarion lead with named-leadership credibility.)

**7. AI positioning (rising across the board).** Name the AI sub-services explicitly:
**AI agents / agentic AI, RAG over your data, generative AI, custom LLMs, AI in
software, MLOps, AI strategy consulting.** Quantify speed gains (2–3× faster). Pre-empt two
buyer questions: *"Can it integrate with our existing stack (SAP/Oracle/ERP)?"* and *"Is our
data secure?"* OpenXcell, Hidden Brains, Clarion, Daffodil all front-load AI.

**8. Differentiators worth stealing (concrete, not generic).**
- **You own the code / IP / no vendor lock-in** (Radixweb, Mindbowser).
- **Custom = one-time cost vs recurring license; ROI in 3–5 yrs** (Radixweb).
- **Extension of your team / dedicated pre-vetted developers, top 1–5%** (Simform, Clarion).
- **Discovery before a single line of code** (Simform, ScienceSoft).
- **50%+ senior engineers** / proactive cost-saving architecture (ScienceSoft).
- **Prebuilt frameworks / accelerators** for faster delivery (Mindbowser).
- **Transparent pricing, no hidden costs** (Mindbowser).
- **Technology-agnostic — right solution, not easiest** (Net Solutions).

**9. Quantification.** The most credible sites attach a number to every claim
(−60% errors, 3× faster, 99% satisfaction, 98% trust score, 90+ NPS, 85% less manual work).
Vague adjectives ("high quality," "innovative") are always backed by a metric or a named client.

**10. CTAs & low-friction conversion.** Primary: "Book a free consultation / Get a quote."
Secondary low-friction tools seen: **ROI calculator, risk-free 2-week trial, POC workshop,
"Talk to an engineer not a salesperson," AI scope builder, on-site AI assistant.** Per-service
"Discuss your project →" arrow links. WhatsApp/chat for instant contact.

**11. Visual / layout style.** Light, spacious, corporate-modern; one accent colour;
soft-shadow cards with hover lift; icon-led service cards (hover changes icon fill); a dark
"band" section for contrast (industries/tech/CTA); subtle background texture (dots/gradients);
generous section padding; consistent eyebrow → heading → sub-copy rhythm; fully responsive
with mobile hamburger + stacked grids.

## PART C — Applied to DotLabs (decisions for this build)

- **Keep the existing multi-page routing** (Services, Industries, Portfolio, Technologies,
  About, Blog, Careers, Contact already have real routes) — but **give each page genuinely
  dedicated, expanded content** instead of re-rendering the homepage's section block. The
  homepage becomes a true overview that teases and links to each deep page (the TatvaSoft pattern).
- **Services page:** expand every service into what-it-is + what's-included + ideal-for +
  tech, plus an **engagement-models** section (fixed-price / T&M / dedicated team) and process.
- **Industries page:** expand each vertical into a longer description + "what we build" +
  compliance/challenge note.
- **Technologies page:** add a *rationale per stack category* (why we choose it) +
  architecture principles, not just chips.
- **About page:** add an expanded story, approach/mission, **milestones timeline**, values,
  leadership/team, engagement note. (Leadership bios flagged as info-needed.)
- **AI front-and-centre** (per OpenXcell/Hidden Brains): agents, RAG, GenAI, LLMs, AI-in-
  software as named services; quantify; address integration + security.
- **Differentiators to feature:** source-code/IP ownership, senior accountable team,
  discovery-before-code, transparent fixed estimates, prebuilt accelerators, long-term
  partnership, security-first.
- **Conversion:** keep "Book a free consultation" + WhatsApp; "talk to an engineer, not a
  salesperson" line; per-service "Discuss your project →".
- **Preserve the visual system** (dot motif, cobalt→cyan gradient, dark contrast bands,
  soft cards) and **full mobile/tablet/desktop responsiveness** (mobile-first Tailwind grids).
